Welcome to newtFire

newtFire serves as an incubator of learning and research collaboration between scholars and students in the Digital Humanities from multiple institutions, and is the project development site of Elisa Beshero-Bondar. Several active projects begun before 2020 are led and maintained by faculty, students, or alumni of the University of Pittsburgh at Greensburg, and several reflect collaborations across multiple institutions. After 2020, new projects and collaborations will reflect Digital Humanities work at Penn State Erie, the Behrend College.

Ongoing projects include:

  • Amadis in Translation
  • Frankenstein Variorum
  • Digital Mitford Project
  • Digital Humanities Courses
  • Thalaba Place Networks

The following featured projects began or developed in Pitt-Greensburg's coding courses:

  • The Banksy Project
  • Emily Dickinson: Fascicles 16 and 6
  • The Hamilton Project
  • The Lope de Vega Project
  • Nell Nelson: City Slave Girls
  • Digital Archives and Pacific Cultures
